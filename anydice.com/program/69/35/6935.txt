\make note that at seventh level the Sorc only gets 7 sorc points, so only gets 3 quickened spells until it must use a bonus action to replenish those. In a day, assuming you use all level 3 spell slots for Haste, you could have up to 30 sorc points total (using multiclass rules). 30/2=15 uses of quickened spells (more than enough for a single day)Also, the bonus action from haste can only be used for the attack action, not cast a spell. Also not to mention, you get even more damage from your superiority dice... I will include that now...Too bad champion and battle master only work on weapon attacks though. Spell Slots: 1st: 4, 2nd: 3, 3rd: 3, 4th 1. Also, you have 2 spell slots of 1st level from Pact magic, ***which replenish on a short rest (effectively giving you an extra 8 sorcery points)*** You could go 5 levels in lock for a higher level Pact Magic spell slot to get more quickened spells, but it may not worth it as you'd lose your third fighter attack on haste (a whole 1d10+5) may be worth it if you wanna pick up pact of the chain and another evocation. If you're asking, "why not go 3 in sorc and put more into lock?" It's because you can only carry so many sorcerer points at one time before you have to spend a bonus action to replenish them, in this case you get seven, so about every three turns, you forgo doing the extra damage in order to replenish some of your sorcery points back to full. Level 7 Sorc is a good balance as you will be able to expend a level 2 spell for 3 more points, bringing you to 4, effectively giving you 5 turns of extreme damage per combat. Ideally, you would expend level 1 spell slots to replenish your points OUTSIDE of combat (as you can only expel 1 spell slot per bonus action) Basically, for one turn you forgo a quickened spell to replenish your points that allow you to use quickened spell.\

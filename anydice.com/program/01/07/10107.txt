\ BLACK CURVE: 6d9                                                                                            \
\ For the probabilities associated with rolling a d9 (an imaginary die with 9 sides) 6 times, or rolling 6d9, \
\ the resulting numbers create a decent bell curve centered on 30 and with a range of 6 to 54. Since the      \
\ result is a combination of 6 numbers from 1 to 9, it gravitates toward the mean, just like how rolling 2d6  \
\ gives a larger chance to get 7 as a result.                                                                 \

\ YELLOW CURVE: Highest Roll                                                                                  \
\ The second graph displays the probability that X is the highest number rolled among the six dice rolls of   \
\ 6d9. The chance of rolling at least one 9 is 50.67%.                                                        \

\ The probability of getting at least 30 from 6d9 (mean value) is 53.07%.                                     \
\ The probability of rolling at least one 9 out of 6d9 is 50.67%.                                             \
\ As such, the probability of having both a total of at least 30 and rolling at least one 9 out of 6 rolls is \
\ a multiplication of the two, resulting in 26.89%.                                                           \

\ 53.07% * 50.67% = 26.89%                                                                                    \

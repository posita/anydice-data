MELEE: Kyll, Rilth, Monk, Namtoo
GUNS: Zephyr, Cayenne, Kip, Rahchet,
MAGIC: Buster, Cedric, Vervain (speculative)
#1: Zephyr
Friendly fire. What the fuck. Not to mention +11 to hit and higher damage than anyone else at all times. And the best AC and highest health. You don't need to give him everything, you know... Or, maybe you do, cause he doesn't know how to add 3 and 3. Does poorly against high AC, but that's pretty much it.
#2: Kyll Gorr
5 punches per turn forever, with one being stunning strike. His only AoE move deals a pitiful 2d6 damage, but other than that, he should never lose a straight fight.
#3: Buster
Mediocre damage, poor survivability, great support. His damaging moves stun and set up advantage, and while enlarge is lackluster for damage, it can be useful if you get creative, and haste is good all the time. He relies fully on spells, but he has so much shit he never runs out anyways. If he had shield and vitriolic sphere, his survivability and damage would be jacked way up, but... do I really wanna ruin everything with a single spell when Artemis has been so nice? Silvery barbs is more interesting than shield ever was.
#4: Cedric
Even as a pacifist, he can pull off a surprising amount of damage, and all his buffs get great mileage. The only thing really keeping him from being better is how limited his resources are... 8 spell points and 3 divinity uses.
#5: Rilrith
Below average damage, but great survivability. With rage and the wall, she reduces most common damage to below half. She has great support with bloodlust, as long as the team sticks close to her. Plus, with all her damage buffs, wicked bite will let her deal almost double damage once per day with a chance to frighten. Even still, buster could totally kick her ass if he wasn't dead.
#6: Rahchet
His gun is powerful, but he's not a good shot, and his spells are powerful, but he's not a good spellcaster. Web is really good, but it also kneecaps his own damage output, since any fire damage burns the webs away. Eldritch cannon is the only thing keeping him above Cayenne, because 2d8 free damage is always nice.
#7: Cayenne
If she was level 5, she would be right above buster. She has the highest burst damage of anybody, but is also the most fragile. Her and Buster make a good team, but in isolation, she's not as great.
#8: Monk
He does better damage than you expect, and every hit can knock the enemy prone, which is fantastic for support. The only problem is his abysmal 20 hit points, but, buster can just re-summon him whenever he gets sneezed on.
#9: Namtoo Gorr
Namtoo has low damage, low health, and no extra effects. Frenzy and power attack should help mitigate this, but frenzy only takes him so far and power attack isn't worth it on average. He'd be pretty good in a vanilla campaign, still. Plus, this goes beyond judging him in isolation, but he's dumb as a box of rocks and almost got killed by piranhas. If he had the fullblade, he could do more damage than rilrith, but he still wouldn't be as good as her. Rilrith has chants, and wicked bite, and combat superiority for some reason, and the combat superiority is better than it should be, and... yeah, man, artemis gets carried away with the npc lackeys.
#10: Kip
Kip has one REALLY good combo, with cunning action + spring attack giving him a totally free, damage boosted attack four times a day, but since Artemis is the one controlling him, he'll never get to do this. Outside of that, he does the worst damage and has low health, and is only really good for knocking people prone, which monk does better. He's a cleric, rogue, and fighter all at the same time, but not smart enough to be good at any.
#11: Vervain
Alright man, I don't know what she would actually have as a character, but every warlock except hexblade fucking sucks. TWO spells? and then we have to do nothing for an hour just so she can do another 3d8 damage? Cayenne is better mechanically and characteristically, dirge can have this flirty bitch. Even if she was a hexblade, she would need some crazy custom weapon to keep up with the rest of the group at this point. I mean, Rilrith's sword is literally just the maul except twice as good. The basic revolver does more damage than most magic weapons, and even the ever-mocked blowback pistols let you attack an extra time with longsword damage.

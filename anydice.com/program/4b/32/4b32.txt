\Due to the wording of Great Weapon Fighting, it has an incredibly enhanced effect on Greatswords compared to other weapons.  
In this example we can see that GWF increases the average damage per swing of a greatsword by 1.33, compared to the .83 it increases a greataxe.
The real kicker is in the change to the damage distribution of the greatsword.  If you click the graph tab below you can see the drastic upward shift
in the normal damage distribution.  Without GWF, you can see that 40% of your hits will do less than the average 7 damage. WITH GWF however, only 33% of your
damage rolls will be below the average.  That means that almost 70% of your swings will be hitting for 8 damage or higher!  While this graph only shows the
average damage going up by a small amount, the near elimination of rolling anything less than a 6 will account for a lot of damage over the course of a game.
Traditionally, a greatsword and a greataxe were comparable, with a greatsword rolling a large percentage of it's rolls in the average range, when compared 
to the greataxe after factoring in GWF however, we can see a stark contrast where the greatsword rolls below 6 far less, and rolls above 6 far more, completely
unbalancing this dynamic. \

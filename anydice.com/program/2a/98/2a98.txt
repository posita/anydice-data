\Each wound is calculated as a die with it's distribution of results being either 0 or 1 dependent on the attacks, chance to hit and chance to wound. 
As given, the initial value "4d" refers to 4 of these dice being rolled.
The next value, "[highest 1 of Xd6]>Y" is the probability of a hit. When there is a re-roll, X=2, otherwise X=1. Y refers to the dice score that you need to be above to hit (one less than the to hit value). In the example, Y is 2 meaning a 3+ to hit
The final value "d6>Z" is the probability of wounding where Z is the value needed to beat in order to hit (one less than the to hit value). In the example, Z=1

If you would like to calculate probabilities for different comparisons of weapon skills and toughnesses, insert the corresponding to hit or wound score into the given formulas\

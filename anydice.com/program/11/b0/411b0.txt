\variables\
N:10 \number of dice\
S:4 \number of sides\
A:1 \number of advantages\
D:0 \number of disadvantages\
\note: when both advantage and disadvantage are used together, they don't cancel out, but their effects combine. Advantage effects add additional dice and the drop low dice, whereas disadvantage effects add additional dice and then drop high dice, so combing them would move the results more towards the centre. If you wish to cancel out advantage and disadvantage, do so for yourself first and only enter the remaining (dis)advantage, putting the other at 0.\

\Formulas:\

\Method 1: additive bonus/penalty die (dis)advantage. - Roll (N+A+D)dS, drop lowest A and drop highest D\
output {1+D..N+D}@(N+A+D)dS named "Method 1: additive bonus/penalty die (dis)advantage" 

\Method 2: multiplicative bonus/penalty dice (dis)advantage. - Roll ((1+A+D)*N)dS, drop lowest A*N and drop highest D*N\
output {(1+N*D)..(N+N*D)}@((1+A+D)*N)dS named "Method 2: multiplicative bonus/penalty dice (dis)advantage"

\Method 3: individual reroll (dis)advantage. - individually roll each die from the NdS set (1+A+D) times, drop the lowest A and drop the highest D and notes the result for that individual die; once all individual dice are determined, add their values together\
output Nd((1+D)@(1+A+D)dS) named "method 3: individual reroll (dis)advantage"

\Method 4: total reroll (dis)advantage. - roll the entire set of NdS 1+A+S times, noting each total separately, drop the lowest A totals and drop the highest D totals\
output (1+D)@((1+A+D)d(NdS))  named "method 4: total reroll (dis)advantage"

\Baseline: no (dis)advantage. - just roll NdS, no (dis)advantage here, this is just there to have the baseline to compare to\
output NdS named "baseline: no (dis)advantage"

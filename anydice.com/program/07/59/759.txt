\ Question:

Hi i have a problem that i've trying to figure out here it is: a fair eight sided die, with faces numbered 1,2...8 is rolled until four even numbers are rolled ina row or until an odd number is rolled followed by three even numbers in a row. if the rolling of the die ends because four even numbers are rolled in a row, then you lose the game. if the rolling of the die ends because an odd number is rolled followed by three even numbers in a row, then you win the game. what is the probability of you winning the game?

Answer:

As you only care about even/odd, you're effectively doing coin flips. Let's call evens 0 and odds 1.

The only way to lose is to get four 0s in a row right from the start. That is because once a 1 is rolled, you win as soon as three 0s are rolled afterwards. So you'll never get to a fourth 0. Rolling a 1 resets the path to victory, but you'll never get back to the path of defeat.

So as soon as a 1 is rolled, it might take some time, but you're guaranteed to win. Thus the odds of winning are equal to not rolling four 0s, which is 1 - 1/2 ^ 4.

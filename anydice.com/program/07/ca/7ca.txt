\Mages really shouldn't be binding. At all. Not with the rules in SR4. The former probability uses the normal method for calculating conjuration Drain, where the Drain DV is equal to 2x the number of hits the Spirit gets to resist being bound. The latter uses Street Magic's alternate rule, where the Drain DV equals half the Spirit's Force plus the number of hits it scores. Just to show that the former rule is still kind of pathetic, there's another spread between the two, using the former's ruleset with an Edge reroll.\

DRAIN: 12
TRACK: 11
FORCE: 6

function:edge DICE:n EDGE:n {
  REROLL: DICE - EDGE
  result: EDGE + REROLLd{0,0,1}
}

output [lowest of [highest of ((FORCE*2)d{0,0,1})*2-(DRAINd{0,0,1}) and 0] and TRACK] named "Force [FORCE] resisted with [DRAIN] dice"
output [lowest of [highest of ((FORCE*2)d{0,0,1})*2-[edge DRAIN DRAINd{0,0,1}] and 0] and TRACK] named "Force [FORCE] resisted with [DRAIN] dice, rerolled with Edge"
output [lowest of [highest of ((FORCE*2)d{0,0,1})-(DRAINd{0,0,1}) and 0] and TRACK] named "Force [FORCE] alt resisted with [DRAIN] dice"
